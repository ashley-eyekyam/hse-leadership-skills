## Scope & route determination

Midland Metro is a non-mainline (street-running tram) passenger operation. Under ROGS 2006
(SI 2006/599) the route follows the dutyholder type:

| Dutyholder type | ROGS route |
|---|---|
| Mainline transport operator | Safety certificate |
| Mainline infrastructure manager | Safety authorisation |
| **Non-mainline (tram / metro / heritage)** ← *this operation* | **Part 3 safety verification** |

This SMS is built to the ROGS/ORR element set and supports the Part 3 safety verification. It
is submitted **for acceptance**.

## 1. Safety policy & objectives

The operation commits to running the light-rail system so far as is reasonably practicable
(SFAIRP), with measurable objectives: zero tram-stop overruns into a conflict zone, a
year-on-year reduction in street-running pedestrian/road-interface conflicts, and 100%
competence currency for safety-critical roles. Owned by the **Accountable Duty-Holder**.

## 3. Risk-control arrangements (hierarchy of controls applied — SFAIRP)

**Significant risk A — tram / road-pedestrian interface conflict (High).** Shared
street-running corridors create a struck-pedestrian / road-vehicle collision risk.

| Control | Tier | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Segregate the highest-conflict corridor from road traffic where reasonably practicable (dedicated alignment) | Elimination | Infrastructure Lead |
| Signal-priority + interlocked pedestrian phases at the worst crossings | Engineering | Engineering Lead |
| Approach-speed restriction + driver street-running procedure | Administrative | Operations Manager |

**Significant risk B — tram-stop overrun on the descending gradient (Medium).** Wet-rail
braking margin on the gradient approach to two stops.

| Control | Tier | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Sanding / enhanced rail-conditioning + automatic-braking enforcement on the gradient approach | Engineering | Engineering Lead |
| Wet-rail driving procedure + approach-speed restriction | Administrative | Operations Manager |

No street-running conflict is left to a "drivers take care" administrative control alone
where a segregation or braking-enforcement (higher-order) control is reasonably practicable.
Residual risk is re-scored after controls on the 5×5 matrix and tracked to the assurance log.

## 4. CSM-RA change interface

A proposed extension to a new street-running section is a **significant change**, so the
CSM-RA significance test applies (Reg (EU) 402/2013, retained EU law in GB):

- **Significance test:** the extension is safety-relevant, partly novel, and not readily
  reversible → significant.
- **Risk-acceptance principle:** demonstrated via **reference systems** (the existing
  comparable street-running corridor) plus **explicit risk estimation** for the new
  pedestrian interface.
- **Independent AsBo:** an independent Assessment Body will assess the correct application of
  CSM-RA for the extension; a hazard record is maintained through the change lifecycle.

## 5. Competence & Sentinel

Tram drivers and safety-critical roles hold current competence on the competence register.
Fitness-for-duty is managed via the confidential occupational-health pathway — individual
medical detail is held in a separate confidential record, not in this SMS. Sentinel does not
apply to this non-mainline operation; an equivalent tram-driver competence register is
maintained and audited by the **Competence Manager**.

## 6. Asset & ECM maintenance

Vehicle and infrastructure condition are managed under a planned maintenance regime; the
Entity in Charge of Maintenance arrangements for the tram fleet are documented and the
maintenance records are auditable. Owned by the **Engineering Lead**.

## 7. Emergency arrangements

Emergency preparedness covers evacuation at street-running stops, overhead-line isolation,
and coordination with the local emergency services; exercise records are retained and the
last exercise informed the corridor segregation case above.

## 8. Monitoring, audit & review

Monitored via near-miss reporting, a rolling internal audit programme, and an annual
management review. Review cadence: **annual or on significant change, whichever is first.**

## 9. Continuous improvement

Incident learning and audit findings feed a continuous-improvement log; corrective actions
are tracked to closure with role owners and due dates.
