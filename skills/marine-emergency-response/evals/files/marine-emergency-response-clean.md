## Lead posture — escalation prevention and TR integrity (not "muster and wait")

The proposed plan reduced the response to "muster to the TR and await evacuation". That
muster-and-wait posture is rejected: the lead controls are escalation prevention (detection,
isolation, firefighting to stop the event reaching the TR) and holding the temporary refuge
for its required endurance so that abandonment remains the last resort, not the headline.

- Escalation prevention — detection, ESD/isolation and active fire protection to keep the
  event from impairing the TR (the LEAD control).
- Temporary-refuge integrity — the TR is shown to survive the dimensioning event for its
  stated endurance; endurance basis recorded, or [GAP] where unsupplied.
- Evacuation — controlled abandonment by TEMPSC only when escalation control and TR
  integrity are exhausted.
- Escape — secondary means (escape routes, scramble nets, life rafts) as defence-in-depth.
- Rescue & recovery — ERRV/standby vessel and reg-17 recovery & rescue arrangements.
