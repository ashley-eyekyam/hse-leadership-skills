# Marine/Offshore Emergency Response (EER) Plan — Thistle Alpha platform

**Installation:** Thistle Alpha (fixed production platform)
**Persons-on-board (reference shift):** 118 (count)
**Scope:** muster + POB accounting · temporary-refuge (TR) integrity · evacuation/escape/rescue hierarchy · TEMPSC/survival-craft · man-overboard (MOB) recovery · helideck
**Classification:** Internal — competent-person (marine/offshore-EER SME) review required
**De-identification:** The station bill assigns every duty by role label (OIM, muster controller, TEMPSC coxswain, ERRV master). Names, OPITO/BOSIET certificate numbers and contacts were pseudonymised before drafting; POB is carried as a count; the re-identification key is held separately.

---

## 1. Lead posture — escalation prevention and temporary-refuge integrity

The plan as proposed reduced the response to *"on any major alarm everyone musters to the temporary refuge and waits for evacuation by lifeboat."* That muster-and-wait posture is **rejected**. The lead controls for every scenario are:

1. **Escalation prevention** — fire & gas detection, ESD/isolation and active fire protection that keep the event from reaching and impairing the temporary refuge. This is the lead control, not an afterthought.
2. **Temporary-refuge integrity** — the TR must be demonstrated to survive the dimensioning event for its required endurance, so the response decision can be made under protection.

Abandonment by TEMPSC is the **last resort**, taken only when escalation control and TR integrity are exhausted — it is never the headline of the plan.

## 2. EER hierarchy (escalation control first, abandonment last)

| Order | Control | Tier | Owner (role) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead | Escalation prevention — detection / ESD / active fire protection to protect the TR | Engineering | OIM |
| Lead | Temporary-refuge integrity for the stated endurance | Engineering | Technical Authority |
| Then | Controlled evacuation — TEMPSC, capacity ≥ POB (see §3) | Substitution | TEMPSC coxswain |
| Then | Escape — secondary means (routes, scramble nets, life rafts) | Administrative | Muster controller |
| Then | Rescue & recovery — ERRV/standby vessel + reg-17 / fast-rescue craft | Administrative | ERRV master |

## 3. TEMPSC / survival-craft capacity vs POB

Survival-craft capacity is checked against POB and must be shown **≥ POB** — it is never assumed adequate.

| Provision | Rated capacity | POB | Sufficiency |
|---|---|---|---|
| TEMPSC (2 × 50) | 100 | 118 | **Shortfall of 18** — finding + SMART action |
| Secondary escape / life rafts | [GAP] — not supplied | 118 | [GAP] — confirm secondary capacity |

The 100 < 118 shortfall is the dominant finding: the abandonment provision is **not** demonstrated adequate for the reference POB.

## 4. Muster, POB accounting & station bill

- Muster to the TR with a defined headcount/POB accounting method; every person carries a station-bill duty by **role label**.
- POB is reconciled against the accounting method at muster; an unaccounted person triggers the search/rescue branch.
- The circulated station bill carries **no name, certificate number, phone or re-identification key**.
