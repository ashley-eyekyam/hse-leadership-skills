# Site Health & Safety Induction Pack — Tilbury Distribution Warehouse

**Audience:** Permanent new starters — warehouse operatives (and shift supervisors).
**Site:** Tilbury distribution warehouse (warehouse & logistics). **Delivery:** Blended.
**Legal-induction baseline:** UK — MHSWR 1999 reg. 10 (information for employees) & reg. 13 (capabilities & training); ISO 45001:2018 cl. 7.2 (competence) & 7.3 (awareness).
**Verification level required:** Trained (operatives) / Competent (supervisors) on `KB-DATA-COMPETENCE-LEVELS`, by supervised sign-off. **Induction owner:** Warehouse HSE Lead. **Refreshers:** Annual.

> **Disclaimer.** Decision-support output. Must be reviewed and signed off by a competent person before use. It does NOT certify any individual as inducted and is not legal advice.

## 2. Induction content — baseline + this site's specifics
The mandatory induction baseline (`KB-SNIP-INDUCTION-BASELINE`) is laid, and **every topic is tied to a named Tilbury arrangement** — not generic boilerplate:

| Baseline topic | Tilbury-specific arrangement |
|---|---|
| Emergency arrangements | Continuous-tone alarm; evacuation via the marked yard route; **named muster point: the front gatehouse car park**; on discovering a fire/incident, raise the alarm and report to the gatehouse marshal (ties to `emergency-response-plan` #15). |

| Incident & concern reporting | Report any hazard, near-miss, or concern to the supervisor or via the gatehouse log; **no-blame** expectation — reporting is expected, not penalised. |

## 3. Site hazards & controls summary (hierarchy of controls)
The named site hazards, each control ranked highest-order first per `KB-SNIP-HOC` — not PPE-only:

| Site hazard | Tier | Control | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| FLT / pedestrian interaction (yard) | Elimination | Physical pedestrian/FLT **segregation** — barriered walkways, separate gates | Site Manager |
| FLT / pedestrian interaction (yard) | Administrative | One-way FLT traffic routes + marked crossing points + 5 mph limit | Warehouse HSE Lead |
| FLT / pedestrian interaction (yard) | PPE | Hi-vis in the yard (only atop the higher-order controls) | Warehouse HSE Lead |
| Mezzanine edge | Engineering | Fixed edge protection + gated goods lift; no working at the open edge | Facilities Lead |
| Manual handling of palletised goods | Engineering | Mechanical handling aids (pump trucks, conveyors) | Facilities Lead |
| Manual handling of palletised goods | Administrative | Team-lift rule above the assessed weight; manual-handling technique in induction | Warehouse HSE Lead |

No hazard is treated PPE-only without a higher-order control above it.

## 4. Emergency & welfare arrangements (this site)
- **Alarm & evacuation** — continuous tone; leave by the nearest marked yard route.
- **Muster point** — the **front gatehouse car park** (named, not generic).
- **First aid** — shift first-aiders (gatehouse rota); first-aid room by the canteen.
- **Welfare** — facilities on the mezzanine landing; report ill-health to the supervisor.
- **On discovering an incident** — raise the alarm, report to the gatehouse marshal.

## 7. Specificity note (warehouse ≠ oil terminal)
This is a **Tilbury warehouse** induction: its named hazards are FLT/pedestrian, mezzanine edge, and manual handling; its muster point is the gatehouse car park. An **oil-terminal** induction would carry different named hazards (flammable atmospheres, confined-space entry, hot-work permits, marine interface) and different arrangements — the two packs are not interchangeable. A pack that would read identically for any site would fail the specificity gate.
