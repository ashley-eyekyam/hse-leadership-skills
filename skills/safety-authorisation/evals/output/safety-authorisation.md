## 3. Risk-control summary (hierarchy of controls applied — SFAIRP)

**Significant risk A — track-worker exposure to running lines during maintenance (High).**
Maintenance access exposes track workers to running-line movements where separation is not
yet engineered. *(SMS §3 risk-control arrangements, referenced.)*

| Control | Tier | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Plan possessions / line-blockages to separate workers from running lines where reasonably practicable | Elimination | Possession Planner |
| Train-warning / TCOD systems where full separation is impractical | Engineering | Engineering Lead |
| Safe-system-of-work briefings + red-zone minimisation procedure | Administrative | Operations Manager |

**Significant risk B — signalling-asset degradation at the oldest interlocking (Medium).** An
ageing interlocking carries a degraded-mode operating risk. *(SMS §6 asset/ECM maintenance,
referenced.)*

| Control | Tier | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Interlocking renewal programme (re-signalling) | Engineering | Engineering Lead |
| Degraded-mode operating procedure + competence refresh | Administrative | Operations Manager |

No track-worker task is left to a lookout-only administrative control where a separation or
train-warning (higher-order) control is reasonably practicable (SFAIRP). The summary draws on
NV-SMS v4.2; it does not re-author it.

## 4. CSM-RA change evidence

The proposed **re-signalling of the oldest interlocking** is a **significant change**, so the
CSM-RA significance test applies (Reg (EU) 402/2013, retained EU law in GB):

- **Significance test:** safety-relevant, partly novel, and not readily reversible →
  significant.
- **Risk-acceptance principle:** demonstrated via **reference systems** (a comparable
  re-signalled interlocking elsewhere on the network) plus **explicit risk estimation** for
  the degraded-mode transition.
- **Independent AsBo:** an independent Assessment Body will assess the correct application of
  CSM-RA for the re-signalling; a hazard record is maintained through the change lifecycle.
