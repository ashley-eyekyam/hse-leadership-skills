## 2. Policy statement

AcmeCo Ltd is committed to protecting the health and safety of its workers, contractors, and visitors across its two warehouses and chemical store. This policy is **appropriate to the size and context** of AcmeCo (~140 staff, multi-site) and the **specific nature of its significant OH&S risks** — warehouse traffic, solvent exposure, and lone driving — and sets the framework within which the organisation manages those risks.

## 3. The five mandatory clause-5.2 commitments (context-fit to AcmeCo's risks)

| # | Clause-5.2 commitment | Context-fit to AcmeCo's actual risk |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **A framework for setting OH&S objectives** | Annual measurable objectives derived from the three significant risks — e.g. a target reduction in forklift/pedestrian near-misses and in solvent-exposure incidents; a lone-driver journey-management objective. |
| 2 | **Fulfil legal and other requirements** | Comply with HSWA 1974 and its regulations (PUWER, COSHH for the solvents, the Work at Height and workplace-transport requirements), maintain ISO 45001 certification scope, and meet customer codes. |
| 3 | **Eliminate hazards and reduce OH&S risks** | Ranked up the hierarchy of controls (see §4) — segregate pedestrian/forklift routes and substitute lower-VOC solvents **before** relying on PPE; journey-management to reduce lone-driving exposure. |
| 4 | **Continual improvement of the OH&S management system** | Review incident trends, audit findings, and objective progress at management review; improve the OH&S MS each cycle. |
| 5 | **Consultation and participation of workers** | Consult workers and the **site safety committee** on risk assessments, control selection, and incident learning; workers help shape the controls for the work they do. |

**All five mandatory commitments are present.** A policy missing any one of these does not satisfy clause 5.2 (a `regulatory_citation_accuracy` hard-fail).

## 4. Hazard elimination & risk reduction (hierarchy of controls — never PPE-only)

| Significant risk | Higher-order control (committed) | Tier | Owner (role/title) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Forklift/pedestrian traffic | Segregated pedestrian walkways + marked crossing/hold points in both warehouses | Engineering | Warehouse Operations Manager |
| Solvent exposure (paint line) | Substitute lower-VOC solvents; LEV at the line | Substitution / Engineering | Engineering Manager |
| Lone driving | Journey-management + scheduling to reduce lone exposure; lone-worker check-in | Administrative | Transport Manager |

PPE (e.g. respiratory protection, hi-vis) supplements — never replaces — these higher-order controls. The policy commits to **eliminate/substitute/engineer before administrate before PPE**.
