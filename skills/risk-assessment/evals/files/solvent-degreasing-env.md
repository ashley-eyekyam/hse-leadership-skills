De-identified intake. All personnel are role labels. This case exercises the
ISO 14001 clause 6.1.2 environmental-aspects branch (Decision 8) alongside the safety scope.

## Scope (Q0)
Both — occupational safety AND environmental aspects.

## Jurisdiction (Q1)
UK.

## Industry (Q2)
Manufacturing — surface finishing.

## Task / activity (safety, Q3)
Operating the solvent degreasing line: load part -> spray degrease -> drain -> dry.

## Environmental activity / product / service (Q-E1 — the environmental specificity anchor)
The solvent degreasing line at Plant 3: load -> spray -> drain -> dry, using a
chlorinated solvent.

## Environmental aspects (Q-E2)
- Emissions to air: VOC / solvent vapour from the spray and dry steps.
- Releases to water: spent solvent and rinse water at the drain step.

## Associated environmental impacts (Q-E3)
- Solvent vapour -> air quality degradation / VOC emission (photochemical smog
  precursor).
- Spent solvent -> water contamination if discharged to the works drain.

## Operating condition (Q-E4)
Normal (production spraying) + Abnormal (maintenance drain-down / tank change-out).

## Compliance obligations (Q-E5)
A VOC emission limit under the site's environmental permit and a trade-effluent
discharge consent for the spent-solvent stream.

## Likelihood / severity / matrix (Q7 / Q8 / Q9)

against environmental consequence descriptors — scale/extent of release,
reversibility, duration).

## Assessment type (Q10)
