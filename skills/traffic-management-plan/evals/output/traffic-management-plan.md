## 1. Site layout & access

The **Meadowbank Road** site has two segregated accesses: a **north gate** for HGV deliveries and a
separate **south gate** for operatives. The principal conflict point is the **north loading bay**,
where reversing HGVs cross the operative route to the **welfare cabins** (south). A **footway runs
adjacent to the north hoarding** (public interface). Routes are laid out **suitable, sufficient and
separated** (CDM 2015 Schedule 3) — this is not a generic "site roads" plan.

## 2. Traffic types & volumes (named routes)

| Traffic type | Route | Interface with pedestrians |
|---|---|---|
| HGV deliveries | North gate → loading bay → exit (one-way) | Crosses the operative welfare route at the north bay — **segregated below** |
| Forklift / telehandler | Loading bay → work areas | Shared route — **physically separated below** |
| Operatives / pedestrians | South gate → barriered walkway → work areas / welfare | **Kept off the vehicle area by design** |
| Public (footway) | Adjacent to the north hoarding | **Protected by hoarding + covered way (not a banksman)** |

## 3. Vehicle-pedestrian segregation

> **Segregation-by-design LEADS, NOT a banksman.** The site's proposed **"operatives wear hi-vis
> and a banksman waves the HGVs in"** pedestrian control is **rejected as a PPE/admin-led
> treatment** (`controls.ppe_admin_only = True`, no higher-order control, no justification) and
> **pushed up the hierarchy**: a barriered, segregated pedestrian walkway removes operatives from
> the vehicle area entirely; the banksman is retained **only as the LAST-resort** residual control
> for the final reversing manoeuvre.

| Control | Tier |
|---|---|
| Remove pedestrians from the vehicle area — barriered, segregated walkway from the south gate to the work areas and welfare; **gated crossing** where it must cross a vehicle route | **Elimination** |
| Physical segregation — pedestrian barriers and edge protection along the shared forklift route; separate vehicle / people site access | **Engineering** |
| Signage, speed limit and lighting at the welfare crossing (warning of approach, Schedule 3) | **Administrative** |
| Banksman / traffic marshal — the **LAST resort**, only for the final reversing manoeuvre once the one-way and segregation are designed in | **Administrative** |

## 4. One-way / turning & reversing controls

> **Reversing is ELIMINATED by design, not managed by a banksman.** The HGV route is made
> **one-way** (north gate in, separate exit) with a **turning circle / drive-through loading bay**
> so vehicles do not reverse into the work area. A trained banksman is the last-resort control for
> any residual manoeuvre — never the headline.

- One-way HGV circulation: enter north gate, drive through the loading bay, exit — **no reversing
  into the work area**.
- Turning circle provided so vehicles turn rather than reverse across the operative route.
- Banksman retained only for the final set-down manoeuvre, **after** the one-way and segregation are
  in place.

## 5. Loading / delivery management

- Deliveries **booked into timed windows** to remove queueing and ad-hoc reversing on the highway.
- Dedicated loading / unloading bay and a **holding area off the public road** for early arrivals.
- Routing to and from the bay fixed and signed; **no delivery reverses across a pedestrian route**.

## 6. Speed limits & signage (Schedule 3 — warning of approach)

| Location | Control | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Site-wide | 5 mph speed limit, signed at the gate | Schedule 3 — warning of approach |
| Welfare crossing | Give-way markings, signage, task lighting | Conflict-point control |
| North gate / footway | Vehicle-emerging warning + wheel-wash to keep the highway clean | Public-interface control |

## 7. Public / highway interface

> **The public is NEVER controlled by a banksman alone.** The footway adjacent to the north
> hoarding is protected by a **continuous hoarding and a covered way**; a vehicle-emerging warning
> (and, where the highway authority requires it, the relevant traffic-signing duty) manages the gate
> / footway interface.

- Continuous hoarding + covered way protect the public footway from site traffic and falling
  materials.
- Vehicle-emerging signage and a banksman **assist** the gate egress — but the public segregation is
  **physical, not a banksman**.

## Regulatory basis

- **CDM 2015 Regulation 27** — organise the site so pedestrians and vehicles can move safely.
- **CDM 2015 Schedule 3** — traffic routes: suitable, sufficient and separated, with warning of
  approach.
- **HSE HSG144** — *The safe use of vehicles on construction sites*.
- **ISO 45001 clause 6.1.2 / 8.1.2** — hazard ID and the hierarchy of controls.
- **Segregation-by-design hierarchy** (`KB-SNIP-TRAFFIC-SEGREGATION`): eliminate the conflict /
  design out reversing → one-way & turning → physical segregation → signage / speed / lighting →
  a banksman LAST.
