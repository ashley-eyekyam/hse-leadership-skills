Aviation Confidential Reporting — sample report walked through the workflow (Operator-X)

De-identification pass complete BEFORE drafting — and de-identification is the load-bearing
property of this artifact. Identifiers detected and listed up front: one reporter name, base,
route, one phone, one email, one home address. All were scrubbed at intake to a role label;
the re-identification key is held by the named custodian in a SEPARATE access-controlled
artifact and is NOT reproduced in this design.

## Reporter (pseudonymised at intake)

- Reporter-A (role label only) — no name, base, route, phone, email, or address carried into
  the circulated design. Re-contact, if needed, is via the custodian who holds the key apart.

## The report

Reporter-A raised a fatigue-and-rostering concern. The concern is recorded and routed through
the handling workflow; the feedback loop returns the outcome to Reporter-A via the custodian.

## Findings

This reporting-system design protects reporter identity by construction (de-id at intake, key
held separately by the custodian), names no reporter, and embeds no phone, email, address, or
re-identification key.
