## 1. Scope & ALCRM band (recorded, not recomputed)

This safe system of work covers two adjacent activities at Mill Lane: the **footpath level
crossing** (a user-worked pedestrian crossing today treated with a passive sign) and the
**S&C renewal** on the Down Main (proposed as a red-zone task with a single lookout while
the line stays open).

The dutyholder supplied an ALCRM individual/collective risk band for the crossing. It is
**recorded** below to prioritise the crossing for remediation; it is **not recomputed**, and
no ALCRM threshold values are reproduced (the band values are the licensed RSSB / Network
Rail model output).

- **ALCRM band (recorded):** C2 — used to prioritise the crossing for the remedial hierarchy.

## 2. Level-crossing remedial hierarchy — closure first, signage last

The crossing is currently "managed" with a new STOP, LOOK, LISTEN sign and a passive
decision-aid. **A signage-led treatment is rejected**: closure and grade separation were not
shown to be not reasonably practicable, and a sign leaves pedestrians crossing at rail level.
The remedial hierarchy is applied in order:

| Order | Control | Disposition for Mill Lane | Owner (role) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Closure | Close the crossing / divert the public right of way | Long-lead closure/diversion order pursued in parallel (recorded right of way constrains immediate closure) | Asset / Route Owner (role) |
| 2 — Grade separation | Footbridge so pedestrians do not cross at rail level | **LEAD control** — feasibility & design commissioned | Engineering Lead (role) |
| 3 — Engineering | Obstacle detection / MSL interim protection until the footbridge is in service | Interim engineered control | Engineering Lead (role) |
| 4 — Sighting / signage / admin | Existing sign + sighting | Retained **only** as a supplementary interim measure — never the lead control | Crossing Keeper (role) |

**Rejected:** "manage the Mill Lane crossing with a new sign." Signage is not the lead
control where grade separation (a footbridge) is reasonably practicable.

## 3. Track-worker protection hierarchy — separation first, lookout last

The S&C renewal was proposed as **red-zone working with a single lookout** on an open line.
**A lookout-only-led system is rejected**: a planned line blockage / possession (green-zone
working) is reasonably practicable for a renewal of this scale and removes the train-strike
exposure entirely.

| Order | Control | Disposition for the renewal | Owner (role) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Separation | Planned possession of the Down Main — green-zone working, no trains on the line of work | **LEAD control** | PICOP (role) |
| 2 — Safe system of work | Authorised work package, limits of the possession, COSS briefing | Authorised SSOW for the possession | COSS (role) |
| 3 — Warning | TOWS on the adjacent open line | Defence-in-depth backstop only | Engineering Supervisor (role) |
| 4 — Lookout-only (red zone) | — | **Removed** from the method of work; red-zone working minimised | Lookout (role) |

**Rejected:** "cover the S&C renewal with a lookout." Where a planned possession (green-zone
working) is reasonably practicable, lookout-only working is not the lead control — keep
workers and trains apart.

## 4. Competence (COSS / Sentinel)

## 5. Significant change (CSM-RA)

Installing a footbridge and/or closing the crossing is a **significant change**. The CSM-RA
significance test applies; the change is taken forward under one of the three risk-acceptance
principles with an independent **AsBo** assessment. Change specifics are `[GAP]` until the
feasibility & design is returned.
