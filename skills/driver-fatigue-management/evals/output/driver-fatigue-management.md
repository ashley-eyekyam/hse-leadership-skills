## 2. Hours-of-service compliance (the authoritative finding)

| Standard | Rule | Limit | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| EU 561 | Daily driving | 9 h (10 h ×2/week) | 8.5 h — **PASS** |
| EU 561 | 45-min break after 4.5 h continuous driving (Art. 7) | break ≥ 45 min | **FAIL** — 5.0 h driven continuously with no qualifying break |
| EU 561 | Daily rest (Art. 8) | ≥ 11 h (reducible to 9 h) | 11.0 h — **PASS** |
| EU 561 | Weekly driving | ≤ 56 h | **[GAP]** — weekly figure not supplied |
| EU 561 | Fortnightly driving | ≤ 90 h | **[GAP]** — fortnightly figure not supplied |

**Binding finding:** the 45-min break after 4.5 h of continuous driving (Art. 7) was breached. This
is the authoritative compliance finding and the trigger for the primary control below.

## 3. Advisory fatigue index

Advisory fatigue index: **0.61 — High band** (a transparent weighted-stressor heuristic: driving
load + time-awake + circadian timing, peaking in the Window of Circadian Low ~02:00–06:00). This is
an **advisory** triage metric, not a regulatory threshold and not a validated biomathematical model;
the compliance finding above is the authoritative basis for action.

## 5. Controls (roster redesign first)

| Tier | Control | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Elimination | Redesign the R-204 roster into a **two-driver relay** so the qualifying 45-min break and the 11 h daily rest are built into the schedule (FRMS) — removes the Art. 7 break breach at source | Fleet Planner (role) |
| Substitution | Journey-plan the route to place the rest stop **before** the 4.5 h continuous-driving point | Transport Manager (role) |
| Engineering | In-cab fatigue-detection alerting as a **backstop** on the night leg (not the headline control) | Fleet Engineer (role) |
| Administrative | Fatigue-risk rostering rules + a short driver fatigue briefing as reinforcement | Transport Manager (role) |

The depot's proposed "stay-alert toolbox talk + in-cab drowsiness alarms" treatment is an
**alertness-led control on a roster that forces a break breach** — it is recorded as a FLAG and
pushed up the hierarchy. The break breach is a scheduling problem: it is removed by the relay split,
not by asking the driver to stay sharper.

## 6. Residual risk

After the roster redesign (relay split + journey-planned break), the residual fatigue risk on R-204
is **Low–Medium** (5×5 qualitative framing): the Art. 7 break is built into the schedule, the daily
rest is preserved, and in-cab detection provides a backstop. Residual risk is re-assessed once the
multi-day [GAP]s are closed.
