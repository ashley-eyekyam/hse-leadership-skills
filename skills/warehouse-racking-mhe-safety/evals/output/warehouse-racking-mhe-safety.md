## 1. Installation, scope & the control posture

This assessment covers the APR installation (bays A1–A48) and the goods-in forklift/pedestrian traffic
layout at the Midlands DC. The site's proposed treatment — "inspect carefully, issue hi-vis, brief
everyone to look out for forklifts" — is **not accepted as the headline control**. Racking and traffic
risk are controlled **by design and engineering first**: the SWL-rated configuration and column
protection, the BS EN 15635 inspection regime, and engineered MHE/pedestrian segregation. Hi-vis and
"look out for forklifts" are residual controls only.

## 5. MHE & pedestrian segregation

Goods-in marshalling is shared by counterbalance forklifts and pedestrian pickers and is currently
controlled by **hi-vis and a briefing only — no engineered segregation**. This is flagged and pushed up
the hierarchy: pedestrian-vehicle separation is achieved **by design first** (HSG136 / KB-REG-MHE-PIT) —
a physical barrier line, a one-way forklift route, and a marked + protected pedestrian walkway with a
controlled crossing — **before** hi-vis is treated as anything but residual. Operator competence
(1910.178(l)-equivalent / L117) and pre-use truck inspection are evidenced for all four operators
(role labels). A prior struck-by incident at this crossing (de-identified — a pedestrian picker, role
label, struck at the unsegregated crossing) is the evidence for this finding.
