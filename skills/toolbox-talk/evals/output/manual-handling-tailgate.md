# Tailgate Talk — Manual Handling at the Despatch Bay

**Delivered by:** Supervisor · **Duration:** under 5 minutes · **Crew:** mixed despatch crew

## Why this matters today

This shift we're moving palletised cartons from floor to conveyor at the despatch bay.
Most of our recordable injuries here are bad backs from lifting. This two-minute talk is
about saving yours.

## The hazards on this task

- Lifting cartons up from **floor level** — strain on the lower back.
- **Twisting** while carrying a load.
- Cartons heavier than they look (**8–22 kg**) — a sudden over-reach.

## What we do about it — highest-protection controls first

1. **Bring the load to you (engineering):** use the powered conveyor and the pallet lift
   so cartons come up to waist height — you shouldn't be lifting off the floor at all
   where the kit can do it. *Owner: Despatch Lead.*
2. **Work the task safely (administrative):** team-lift anything over 20 kg; keep the load
   close, feet apart, no twisting — turn with your feet, not your back. *Owner: Supervisor.*
3. **PPE is the LAST line:** grip gloves where carton edges are sharp — after the lift kit
   and the technique above, not instead of them. *Owner: each worker.*

## Learn from this (illustrative example — not a local event)

As a typical case: a picker over-reached for a top-shelf carton, strained their back, and
ended up three weeks off. The lesson — bring the load to you; don't reach for it.

## Let's talk it through

- What's the heaviest thing we lift on this bay, and how should we lift it?
- When do we call for a team lift instead of going solo?

## Three things to take away

- Bring the load to waist height — use the conveyor and the lift.
- Over 20 kg, team-lift; keep it close, feet apart, no twist.
- Tired or sore? Tell the supervisor — we rotate the task.
