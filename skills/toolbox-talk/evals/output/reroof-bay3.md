# Toolbox Talk — Re-roofing Bay 3, MEWP Out of Service

**Delivered by:** Site Supervisor · **Duration:** under 5 minutes · **Crew:** mixed re-roofing crew, Bay 3

## Why we're stopping for two minutes

We are re-roofing Bay 3 this shift and the cherry-picker (MEWP) is out of service —
so we're working off the leading edge by hand. A fall from this roof is around 6 m onto
the slab: life-changing or fatal. This talk is so nobody goes near that edge unprotected.

## The hazards on THIS job (not "working at height" in general)

- **Unprotected leading edge** on the Bay 3 roof — a fall straight to the slab below.
- **The MEWP is out of service**, so our planned safe access isn't there today.
- **Mixed crew** — not everyone has worked this roof profile or knows the edge layout.
- **Dropped objects** — tools and roof sheets near the edge can fall on anyone below.

## What we're doing about it — highest-protection controls first

1. **Get rid of the edge work where we can (eliminate):** pre-assemble roof sections at
   ground level and lift them up as units, so fewer people spend time at the edge.
   *Owner: Site Supervisor.*
2. **Guard the edge before anyone goes near it (engineering):** temporary edge-protection
   guardrail goes up along the Bay 3 working edge first, and gets checked. *Owner: Scaffold Lead.*
3. **Fix the access (engineering / alternative):** with the MEWP down, work stays inside
   the guardrail; any task that must reach the edge is on an anchored harness + running
   line — or it waits until the MEWP is back. *Owner: Working-at-Height Supervisor.*
4. **Control who and what is near the edge (administrative):** permit-to-work, a briefed
   exclusion zone on the slab below, tethered tools. *Owner: Supervisor.*
5. **PPE is the LAST line, never the only line:** hard hats and inspected harnesses — and
   only because the guardrail, access and exclusion-zone controls above are already in
   place. PPE on its own does not make this edge safe. *Owner: each crew member.*

## Learn from this (illustrative example — not a local event)

As a typical case from this kind of job: a worker carrying a sheet stepped back toward an
unguarded edge and was only stopped by a co-worker's shout. The lesson — edge protection
goes up **before** the work starts, not after.

## Let's talk it through

- With the MEWP out of service, what is OUR safe way to reach the edge today?
- Where is the guardrail going, and who checks it before we start?
- If something feels wrong up there — what do you do, and who do you tell?

## Three things to take away

- Nobody approaches the Bay 3 edge until the guardrail is up and checked.
- MEWP is down: edge tasks are harness + running line, or they wait.
- Tools tethered, materials back from the edge, exclusion zone kept clear.
