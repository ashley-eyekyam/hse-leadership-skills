# Toolbox Talk — Manual Handling (short, plain-English tailgate)

A <5-minute plain-English tailgate talk for a mixed crew. Short, frontline-readable,
structured hook → hazards → controls → incident → discussion → takeaways → sign-off,
and it includes a fillable attendance / sign-off table. De-identification holds — role
labels only, no names, no small (<5) injury cells, no re-identification key.

## Why this matters today

We are moving palletised cartons from floor to conveyor at the despatch bay this shift.
Most of our recordable injuries are bad backs from lifting — this two-minute talk is
about saving yours.

## The hazards

- Lifting from floor level — strain on the lower back.
- Twisting while carrying a load.
- Cartons heavier than they look (8–22 kg) — a sudden over-reach.

## Key controls (higher-order first)

- Engineering: use the powered conveyor and the pallet lift to bring the load to waist
  height — owner: Despatch Lead.
- Administrative: team-lift anything over 20 kg; keep the load close, feet apart, no
  twisting — owner: Supervisor.
- PPE (last line): grip gloves where the carton edges are sharp — owner: each worker.

## Learn from this (illustrative / typical example)

A picker on a similar bay strained their back over-reaching for a top-shelf carton —
a near-miss that became three weeks off. The lesson: bring the load to you; do not reach.

## Discussion prompts

- What is the heaviest thing we lift on this bay, and how should we lift it?
- When do we call for a team lift instead of going solo?

## Takeaways

- Bring the load to waist height — use the conveyor and the lift.
- Over 20 kg, team-lift; close to the body, feet apart, no twist.
- Tired or sore? Tell the supervisor — we rotate the task.
