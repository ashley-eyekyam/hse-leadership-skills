# Safety audit input — Compliance audit vs a regulatory regime (thin/absent evidence + an admin-only fix)

De-identification pass complete BEFORE any assessment. All personnel are referred to by role
label; no name, address, or health detail is carried into this circulated input.

## Audit request

- **Scope:** the machinery-guarding compliance regime on the Plant 3 press line — guard
  fitment, isolation-before-maintenance, and statutory examination. **Boundary:** the press
  line only.
- **Criteria (Q-Crit):** a **regulatory regime** — the applicable machinery-guarding and
  examination duties for the jurisdiction (UK; the matched fragment's duties).
- **Audit type:** compliance audit. **Jurisdiction:** UK. **Matrix:** org 5x5.

## Evidence gathered (scrubbed — deliberately mixed quality)

- **C-1 Guard fitment** — **strong evidence:** observation + a maintenance log show fixed
  guards fitted and intact across the sampled presses. (Conformity expected.)
- **C-2 Statutory examination of lifting/pressure equipment** — **thin/absent evidence:**
  no examination certificate was produced for the line's overhead crane; the auditor was
  told "it's done but the paperwork is with head office." No record sampled.
- **C-3 Isolation before maintenance** — **nonconformity:** 3 sampled maintenance jobs were
  done without recorded isolation. The site's **proposed corrective action is administrative
  only — "remind staff to follow the isolation procedure" (a toolbox talk).**

## What the auditor needs

A clause-by-clause compliance audit. Note: do NOT pass C-2 on the verbal assurance — absent
evidence is a nonconformity or "insufficient evidence," never a silent conformity. For C-3,
the admin-only "remind staff" corrective action must be challenged: add a higher-order
control (e.g. an isolation interlock / lock-off-to-isolate engineering control) or record an
explicit justification that higher-order controls are not reasonably practicable. Cite the
regulatory duties for the criteria; risk-rate every nonconformity.
