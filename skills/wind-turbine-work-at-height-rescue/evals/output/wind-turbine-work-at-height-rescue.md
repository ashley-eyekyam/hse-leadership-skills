## 1. The proposed rescue arrangement — and why it is rejected

The rescue arrangement proposed for this climb was: *"if a worker is suspended in their harness, call 999 and the site first-aider will assist until the fire and rescue service arrives."*

This is **rejected** as the rescue plan. Suspension trauma can be fatal within minutes, and an external response cannot be relied on to arrive and reach a worker suspended in a nacelle in that window. Under WAH Regs 2005 **reg 4, rescue planning is part of the work** — not an afterthought delegated to the emergency services. A WAH plan whose lead rescue arrangement is "phone 999 and wait" is the indefensible paperwork this plan replaces.

**The rescue plan is therefore a tested, team-owned, timed capability** (see §4), with emergency services called **in parallel as a supplement only**.

## 2. Avoidance test (reg 6 — avoid work at height first)

The gearbox borescope inspection was tested for a ground-level / remote option: the component cannot be brought to ground without a major lift, and remote borescope access is not feasible for this fault. **Work at height is therefore unavoidable for this task** — the avoidance test is recorded, not assumed. (Owner: WAH Planner (role).)

## 3. Control order (reg 6 / 7 — collective before personal)

| Order | Control | Tier | Owner (role) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead | Avoid work at height — borescope/remote option tested; component cannot be lowered, so at-height work is unavoidable (recorded) | Elimination | WAH Planner |
| Then | Prevent a fall — nacelle internal working platform + roof-hatch guardrail (collective protection) | Engineering | Site WAH Supervisor |
| Then | Mitigate / arrest — harness + energy-absorbing lanyard on the rated nacelle anchor for the roof-hatch transition only | PPE | Climb Lead |

Collective fall-prevention is selected **before** personal fall-arrest (reg 7). A harness/lanyard-only treatment for the roof-hatch transition, where a temporary guardrail is reasonably practicable, would be pushed back up the hierarchy.

## 4. Mandatory tested rescue plan (the core requirement)

| Element | Arrangement | Recovery time | Owner (role) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Suspended-worker recovery | Team-owned rescue descent/haul kit (rescue device + rated anchor) per the GWO ART method, drilled quarterly | 10 min (measured) | Rescue Lead |
| Manning | Two-person-minimum climb team + one ground-support person able to initiate the rescue | Immediate | Climb Lead |
| Emergency services | 999 called in parallel by Ground Support — a SUPPLEMENT, not the rescue plan | External | Ground Support |

The team can recover a suspended worker within minutes using its own competence and equipment. The measured recovery time is verified by a drill on WTG-12 access before the climb; an untested capability or an unstated recovery time would be flagged `[GAP]` and the climb held.
