# Safety Culture Assessment — Northgate Distribution Centre

**Assessment type:** Periodic culture diagnosis (post-restructure baseline), with an advancement roadmap.
**Unit in scope:** Northgate distribution centre — a named culture-bearing population (≈140 staff across Logistics, Maintenance and site leadership). Assessed by cohort; no individual named.
**Model / lens:** Hudson / Parker "Hearts & Minds" ladder (5 rungs) **combined with** the Schein three-levels lens (espoused-vs-enacted gap as a triangulation lens — a diagnosis, not a 4th ladder band).
**Jurisdiction:** UK — HSWA 1974 leadership and worker-participation duties; ISO 45001 clause 5.1 (leadership & commitment) and 5.4 (consultation & participation of workers) are the duties this assessment evidences.
**Method basis:** culture models cited as **method, not law** — Hudson (Leiden/Shell), Westrum (BMJ Qual Saf 2004), Schein (*Organizational Culture and Leadership*); maturity bands resolved from `KB-DATA-CULTURE-MATURITY` (source + year quoted).

> **Disclaimer.** Decision-support output. Must be reviewed and signed off by a competent person (safety-culture / OD specialist) before use. This is a culture diagnosis, not an individual assessment, and not legal advice.

## 1. De-identification status (sensitive personnel data)

De-id pass ran **FIRST**. Survey, leadership-walk and records inputs were treated as **sensitive personnel data in a small population**. Identifiers detected in the raw pack (two respondent names, one home address, one phone number, one identifying verbatim comment) were pseudonymised to **role/group/cohort labels**; the re-identification key is held in a SEPARATE access-controlled artifact and is NOT reproduced here. **No individual is named as the culture problem.** Any department cohort of fewer than 5 respondents was **suppressed** with **secondary suppression** so a suppressed value cannot be back-calculated from totals; no verbatim comment is reproduced in a way that identifies a speaker.

## 4. Schein espoused-vs-enacted gap (diagnosis — not a band)

| Espoused value (level 2) | Enacted assumption (levels 1 & 3) | Gap | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Safety is our first priority" (site charter, induction) | When throughput targets and a safety stop collide, throughput is seen to win on the night shift | **Wide** | Leadership-walk observations + aggregated survey themes |
| "Speak up — every concern is acted on" | Reporting-rate records show concerns raised but slow to close; staff infer raising a concern rarely changes anything | **Moderate** | Reporting-rate records + survey free-text themes (paraphrased) |

The **wide espoused-vs-enacted gap on the safety-stop assumption** is the primary advancement-roadmap input. This is a named-gap diagnosis, **not** a Schein maturity band.

## 5. Systemic drivers (attributed to the leadership system / work design)

- The **consequence system** rewards throughput over a safety stop on the night shift (felt-leadership inconsistent out-of-hours) — a system driver, attributed to the night-shift leadership cohort and the targets regime, never to an individual.
- **Worker consultation under ISO 45001 clause 5.4** is thin: concerns are collected but not visibly closed, suppressing the Westrum information flow.

## 6. Advancement controls (hierarchy of controls — systemic above awareness)

| Advancement control | HoC tier | Owner (role label) |
|---|---|---|
| Close the safety-stop gap: change the night-shift target/consequence rules so a safety stop never disadvantages the team | Elimination | Operations Director |
| Stand up a genuine worker consultation & concern-closure loop (ISO 45001 clause 5.4) with visible close-out | Engineering (system) | HSE Manager |
| Establish a felt-leadership safety-walk cadence covering every shift, including nights | Administrative | Site Leadership cohort |

An awareness / poster campaign alone is explicitly rejected as the primary move — it does not change the enacted assumption.

## 7. Advancement roadmap (SMART, tracked to leading indicators)

| Advancement action | Leading indicator (KB-DATA-LEADING-INDICATORS) | Owner (role label) | Due |
|---|---|---|---|
| Re-write night-shift targets so a safety stop is consequence-neutral | Safety-stop count + % stops with no adverse consequence | Operations Director | 1 Sep 2026 |
| Launch the concern-closure loop with published close-out times | Concern close-out rate; near-miss reporting rate | HSE Manager | 1 Oct 2026 |
| Roll the felt-leadership walk cadence to all shifts | Leadership-walk commitment closure-rate | Site Leadership cohort | 15 Oct 2026 |

Every action has a named role-label owner, an ISO due date, and a measurable leading indicator — advancement is measured, not asserted.
