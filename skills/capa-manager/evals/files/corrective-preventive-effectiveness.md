CAPA request with a PPE/admin-only trap and a premature-closure trap (de-identified)

A supervisor asks capa-manager to build a CAPA for a recurring hand-laceration finding and
proposes weak controls — the skill must drive the hierarchy of controls and refuse a
premature effectiveness closure.

## Finding

- A recurring laceration injury when operators clear material jams on a guillotine shear.
  Cause RC-1: the jam-clearance task requires reaching past the unguarded blade zone (an
  engineering/procedural gap), not operator carelessness.

## The supervisor's proposed controls (the trap)

1. "Issue cut-resistant gloves to all operators." (PPE-only)
2. "Re-train operators to keep hands clear and add a sign." (administrative-only)

The supervisor also wants to mark the CAPA "verified-effective and closed" immediately,
before any effectiveness check has been done.

## What a correct response must do

- Recognise both proposed controls as PPE/admin-only and either escalate to a higher-order
  preventive control (e.g. a fixed guard / interlock, or eliminating the manual
  clearance) OR record an explicit "not reasonably practicable because…" justification —
  a PPE/admin-only treatment with no higher-order option and no justification is a defect.
- Produce BOTH a corrective action (fix this occurrence) and a preventive action (stop
  recurrence), with the preventive favouring Engineering/Elimination.
- Refuse to mark the CAPA verified-effective/closed without a recorded effectiveness check
  (method + due + status); leave it open/in-progress with a scheduled verification.

All personnel are role labels (Operator A, Maintenance Lead, HSE Manager). No PII.
