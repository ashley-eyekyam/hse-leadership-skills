# United Kingdom — CDM 2015 (Construction Design & Management duty map)

**Fragment ID:** `KB-REG-CDM2015`
**What this is:** a copyright-safe **duty → artifact map** for the Construction
(Design and Management) Regulations 2015 (CDM 2015) — the dutyholder roles
(Client, Principal Designer, Designer, Principal Contractor, Contractor), the
pre-construction information / construction phase plan / health & safety file
trio, and the F10 notification trigger.
**What this is NOT:** a reproduction of CDM 2015 or its L153 ACOP text. Cite the
Regulation numbers and the duty topics only — never paste the regulation or ACOP
wording.

> Source: Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/51) + HSE L153 ACOP — citation map · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-21 · Volatile: true (statutory status + guidance drift; refresh on review).

CDM 2015 governs the management of health, safety and welfare on **construction
projects** in Great Britain. It distributes duties across the project team and
requires the project's safety to be planned, managed and documented from concept
through to handover. It grounds construction permit/plan and risk-assessment
skills with the role/artifact framework a UK construction project must produce.

## Dutyholder / trigger → duty → artifact it grounds

| Role / trigger | Duty (paraphrased) | Artifact it grounds |
|---|---|---|
| Client (commercial / domestic) | Make suitable arrangements; appoint PD + PC; provide pre-construction information | appointment record + pre-construction information (PCI) pack |
| Principal Designer (PD) | Plan, manage, monitor the pre-construction phase; coordinate design risk | design risk register + PCI contributions |
| Designer | Eliminate / reduce foreseeable risk through design; provide information | design risk information feeding the PCI |
| Principal Contractor (PC) | Plan, manage, monitor the construction phase; coordinate the CPP | Construction Phase Plan (CPP) |
| Contractor | Plan, manage, monitor own work; comply with PC directions | method statements / task RAs under the CPP |
| Project notifiable (>30 working days & >20 workers, or >500 person-days) | Notify HSE before construction starts | F10 notification |
| Project handover | Compile and pass on residual-risk information | Health & Safety File |

## How the skill uses this fragment
- A UK construction skill resolves the dutyholder role first, then grounds the
  required artifact (PCI / CPP / H&S File / F10) against the duty above.
- The notification threshold is a **trigger test**, not a fixed answer — a skill
  states the test and lets the project facts decide; a missing input is `[GAP]`.
- The hierarchy of controls is applied at the Designer duty (eliminate/reduce by
  design before relying on the CPP's site controls).
