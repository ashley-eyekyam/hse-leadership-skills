# Safety-culture maturity bands + Schein espoused-vs-enacted gap rubric (each cited)

**Fragment ID:** `KB-DATA-CULTURE-MATURITY`
**What this is:** the **maturity-band reference** the `safety-culture-assessment` skill bands
survey/observation results against for the three **ladder** models (Bradley · Hudson) PLUS a **Schein
levels-alignment rubric expressed as espoused-vs-enacted GAP signals — NOT maturity bands** (D-05).
Model descriptors and diagnostic probes live in `KB-SNIP-CULTURE-MODELS`; this fragment carries the
banding/gap detail.
**What this is NOT:** a validated psychometric instrument or a clinical score. A maturity rating is
defensible only with a **named model + named population + ≥2 data sources**; survey responses are
de-identified (small-cell `<5` suppression, no attribution to a nameable individual). Each band names
its method authority — a band is never a bare label. Cited as **method, not law**.

> Source: DuPont Sustainable Solutions (Bradley Curve) · Hudson/Parker "Hearts & Minds" ladder · Edgar H. Schein, *Organizational Culture and Leadership* (three-levels gap) · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-22 · Volatile: true (model descriptors stable; resolve current published wording against the named method source at use time).

## Ladder maturity bands (the three ladders ARE bands)

### Bradley Curve — ownership-of-safety bands

| Band | Where ownership sits | Outward signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Reactive** | the safety manager / instinct | injuries managed after the fact; compliance is the goal. |
| **Dependent** | supervision / rules | rules & supervisor control drive behaviour. |
| **Independent** | the individual | each worker owns their own safety; habits internalised. |
| **Interdependent** | the team | workers are others'-keepers; lowest injury rate, highest ownership. |

### Hudson / Parker — "Hearts & Minds" rungs

`pathological → reactive → calculative → proactive → generative` — band the population on the
four-behaviour grid (leadership thinking · worker action · information flow · incident treatment) in
`KB-SNIP-CULTURE-MODELS`. Advancement = movement **up** the rungs, evidenced across ≥2 sources.

## Schein levels-alignment rubric — espoused-vs-enacted GAP signals (NOT bands) — D-05

Schein is **not** scored as a maturity band. The advancement-roadmap input is the **gap** between the
**espoused** value (Schein level 2) and the **enacted** assumption revealed in artifacts and behaviour
(levels 1 & 3).

| Gap signal | Espoused (level 2) | Enacted (levels 1 & 3) | Roadmap input |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Narrow / aligned** | "we stop unsafe work" | jobs are stopped; stopping is praised | values are lived — sustain. |
| **Wide / misaligned** | "safety is our #1 priority" | production pressure wins when schedule and safety collide | the gap **is** the advancement target. |

**State explicitly in any standalone Schein output:** the result is a **three-levels diagnosis (named
gaps)**, not a maturity rating. There is no "Schein band" — the gap is the finding. (The literal
phrase used as the anti-pattern warning sign, e.g. equating a Schein level to a generative rung, must
never appear; Schein levels are not ladder rungs.)

## Quoting + de-id discipline

- Present every ladder result as `<model> <band> — <method authority>, <year>`, resolved from the
  named method at use time, never a remembered cut-off.
- A team/department breakdown with **fewer than 5 respondents is suppressed** (de-id); never attribute
  a culture finding to a nameable individual.

## How the skill uses this fragment

`safety-culture-assessment` bands each selected ladder model here, triangulates across ≥2 data sources,
and — where Schein is selected — produces an **espoused-vs-enacted gap diagnosis** (not a band) that
feeds the `smart_actions` advancement roadmap.
