# Sharps hierarchy — engineering-first control of sharps & needlestick exposure

**Fragment ID:** `KB-SNIP-SHARPS-HIERARCHY`
**This is prompt text, applied by the model — not a generator.** It is the
hierarchy-of-controls gate for sharps and needlestick exposure in `hse-healthcare`:
**eliminate unnecessary sharps, then engineer (safer devices, point-of-use

the residual lines.** A sharps treatment that leads with "wear gloves and be
careful" is the PPE-led paperwork this gate rejects.

## Instruction — the sharps control order

1. **Eliminate.** Remove sharps where the procedure no longer needs them (needleless
   systems, alternative routes); ban recapping by hand.
2. **Engineer (substitute).** Use medical devices with engineered sharps-injury
   protection (safety-engineered devices); provide point-of-use sharps containers
   so the sharp is disposed at the moment of use.
3. **Administrative.** Safe handling and disposal procedures, no-pass / neutral-zone
   technique in surgery, training, the sharps-injury log, and the annual safer-device
   evaluation with frontline input.
4. **PPE + PEP (residual only).** Gloves/eye protection as the residual barrier, and
   a confidential post-exposure prophylaxis pathway — never as the headline control.

## The gate (reject these)
- A treatment that relies on PPE/"be careful" without engineering and
  work-practice controls → **reject** (PPE-led).
- A device choice that ignores available safety-engineered alternatives → **reject**.
- Reporting an exposure cluster in a way that allows re-identification of a worker
  or source patient → **reject** (apply small-cell suppression).
