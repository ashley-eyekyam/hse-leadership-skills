# Pre-output Quality Checklist — ISO 45001:2018 clause-5.2 OH&S policy

Before producing any output, validate the draft against this gate. Any unchecked item is a
defect to fix before output; the clause-5.2 completeness items are non-waivable
`regulatory_citation_accuracy` hard-fails.

## Clause-5.2 completeness (citation accuracy — non-waivable)
- [ ] **All five mandatory commitments present and named explicitly** (`KB-SNIP-POLICY-COMMITMENTS`): (1) a framework for setting OH&S objectives; (2) fulfilling legal + other requirements; (3) eliminating hazards and reducing OH&S risks; (4) continual improvement of the OH&S MS; (5) consultation and participation of workers (and their representatives).
- [ ] **No commitment silently dropped or merged** — a policy missing any one of the five is a hard-fail, recorded `[GAP]`, never papered over.
- [ ] Commitment (3) is **ranked up the hierarchy of controls** (`KB-SNIP-HOC`: eliminate → substitute → engineer → administrate → PPE) — **never** a PPE-only or admin-only commitment.

## Anti-boilerplate context-fit (the core-value specificity lever)
- [ ] **Every commitment ties to the named organisation's actual risks and scale** — each references a concrete hazard/obligation the org carries (e.g. forklift traffic, solvent exposure, lone driving), taken from intake.
- [ ] **No generic "we are committed to safety" statement** with no named risk — such a draft is refused and the real risk elicited (`[GAP]`), never invented.
- [ ] **Appropriate to purpose, size and context** — the policy scales to the organisation (a 12-person workshop ≠ a 4,000-person multi-site operation).

## Clause-5.2 characteristics
- [ ] Available as **documented information**, **communicated** within the organisation, and **available to interested parties**.
- [ ] **Signed by top management**, with the signatory captured as a **role/title** ("Managing Director"), never personal data.

## Statutory written-policy duty branch (beside the policy, never inside it)
- [ ] The **written-policy legal duty** is resolved as a jurisdiction branch: **UK** HSWA 1974 **s.2(3)** (5+ employees); **India defers to `hse-india`** — **state resolved first**, **no national-form number minted** (`[GAP]` when unverified), and it **never hard-blocks** the policy; US/EU per §5 of METHODOLOGY.

## De-identification (standard tier)
- [ ] De-identification pass ran **BEFORE** drafting; the policy and its named-risk examples carry **no personal data** (signatory and any named role given as title only).

## Boundary held (decision-support framing)
- [ ] Improvement intentions narrated via the hierarchy of controls; each forward action owned + dated (`smart_actions`).
- [ ] The policy **never reads as a final, approved, or legal document** and **never asserts it has been signed off by a competent person** — it is a draft that precedes, never replaces, the organisation's own competent-person review (SME persona: Senior HSE Manager / Director).
