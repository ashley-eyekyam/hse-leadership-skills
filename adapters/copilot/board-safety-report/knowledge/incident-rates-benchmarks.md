# Incident-rate benchmarks (TRIR / DART / LTIFR) — figures with source+year

**Fragment ID:** `KB-DATA-TRIR-BENCHMARKS`
**Discipline:** every figure is presented **with its `source` + `year`** — figures are
never bare (§17). Verify against the cited source before quoting in an artifact; these
are volatile (published annually).

> Volatile: true · last_reviewed: 2026-05-15 (review window 180 days, D-05).

---

## Definitions (formulae are stable; the rates are volatile)

- **TRIR** (Total Recordable Incident Rate) = (recordable cases × 200,000) / total
  hours worked. *Source: OSHA 29 CFR 1904 recordkeeping methodology · Year: 2026.*
- **DART** (Days Away, Restricted, or Transferred) = (DART cases × 200,000) / hours.
  *Source: OSHA 29 CFR 1904 · Year: 2026.*
- **LTIFR** (Lost-Time Injury Frequency Rate) = (lost-time injuries × 1,000,000) /
  hours worked. *Source: ILO / common international convention · Year: 2026.*
- **200,000** = 100 full-time-equivalent workers × 2,000 hours/year (US convention).
  *Source: OSHA recordkeeping · Year: 2026.*

## How to use benchmarks defensibly

Benchmark figures (industry-average TRIR/DART by NAICS sector, national LTIFR) MUST be
quoted with the **publishing body, the reference year, and the sector** — e.g. "US
private-industry all-sector recordable rate, BLS, [year]". Do **not** quote a remembered
number; resolve the current figure from the cited source at use time and present it as:

> `<rate> — <metric>, <sector>, <publishing body>, <year>`

**Primary published sources to cite (resolve the current-year figure at use time):**

| Metric / scope | Publishing body | Cadence | Source line to quote |
|---|---|---|---|
| US recordable & DART rates by industry (NAICS) | US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), Survey of Occupational Injuries and Illnesses | annual | "BLS SOII, [year]" |
| GB/UK injury & ill-health rates | UK Health & Safety Executive (HSE) annual statistics | annual | "HSE statistics, [year]" |
| International / sectoral fatality & injury rates | International Labour Organization (ILO) | periodic | "ILO, [year]" |

> Source: BLS SOII / UK HSE annual statistics / ILO (publishing bodies named above) · Year: 2026 (methodology); the *rate values* are resolved from the current-year publication at use time.
