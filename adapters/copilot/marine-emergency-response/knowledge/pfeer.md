# Offshore PFEER 1995 (SI 1995/743) — fire/explosion & emergency-response duty map

**Fragment ID:** `KB-REG-PFEER`
**What this is:** a copyright-safe **duty → artifact map** for the Offshore
Installations (Prevention of Fire and Explosion, and Emergency Response)
Regulations 1995 (PFEER) — grounding `marine-emergency-response` (MAR-03). It names
muster, temporary-refuge integrity, the EER chain, detection/firefighting and the
reg 17 recovery & rescue duty.
**What this is NOT:** a reproduction of SI 1995/743 or the HSE L65 ACOP text. Cite
the regulation number and the duty topics only.

> Source: Offshore Installations (Prevention of Fire and Explosion, and Emergency Response) Regulations 1995 (SI 1995/743) + HSE L65 ACOP + OEUK/OGUK Emergency Response guidelines — citation map · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-23 · Volatile: true.

PFEER sets the duty-holder's duties for preventing fire/explosion and for
emergency response on offshore installations. It grounds the offshore
emergency-response limb of a marine artifact.

## Duty / clause → duty → artifact it grounds

| Clause / element | Duty (paraphrased) | Artifact it grounds |
|---|---|---|
| Muster arrangements | Provide effective arrangements for mustering and accounting for all persons | muster plan + POB accounting |
| Temporary refuge (TR) integrity | Maintain a TR that protects persons for the required endurance during an emergency | TR integrity basis |
| EER (evacuation, escape, rescue) | Provide for evacuation, escape and rescue from the installation | EER plan |
| Detection & firefighting | Provide fire/gas detection and firefighting arrangements | detection + firefighting controls |
| **Reg 17** recovery & rescue | Make arrangements for recovery and rescue of persons (near/from the installation), incl. ERRV | recovery & rescue arrangements + ERRV (Emergency Response and Rescue Vessel) |

## The emergency hierarchy (escalation-prevention first)
The EER chain is **muster → temporary refuge → evacuation → escape → rescue** —
TR integrity and escalation-prevention come **before** abandonment, never after
(see `KB-SNIP-EER-MUSTER`). An EER plan reduced to "abandon and wait for rescue"
is the indefensible output this map rejects.
