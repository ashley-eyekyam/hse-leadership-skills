# Behaviour-based safety — ABC analysis + non-punitive observation design

**Fragment ID:** `KB-SNIP-BBS-METHOD`
**This is prompt text, applied by the model — not a generator.** It is the method the
`bbs-program-designer` skill applies to design a non-punitive, behaviour-focused observation
programme that trends to **systemic** causes. At-risk behaviours route to **system fixes** (via the
`controls` engine, hierarchy-ranked), **never** to "retrain the worker" or discipline. Defined
metrics + the small-cell guardrail live in `KB-DATA-BBS-METRICS`. Cited as **method, not law**.

## Instruction — ABC (Antecedent–Behaviour–Consequence) analysis

| Element | What it is | Discipline |
|---|---|---|
| **Antecedent** | what triggers/prompts the behaviour (cue, instruction, condition). | identify the real trigger, not a slogan. |
| **Behaviour** | the observable, site-specific act. | must be **observable** — never "work safely"/"be careful". |
| **Consequence** | what follows and reinforces it. | the strongest driver; **soon-certain-positive** consequences dominate. |

**Design principle:** engineer the system so the **safe** behaviour carries the soon/certain/positive
consequence. An at-risk behaviour is a signal of a **system gap** — route it through `controls`
(eliminate/substitute/engineer/admin before PPE), never to discipline or "retrain the worker".

## Non-punitive observation-card design principles

- Items are **observable and site-specific** (not "be careful"); each tied to a named task/area.
- **Role-labelled or anonymous by default** — never records a nameable individual for discipline.
- **Voluntary** participation; data is used for **trending and learning**, never individual sanction.
- The observer→observed feedback is **two-way and immediate** (positive AND at-risk discussed on the
  spot, in the worker's own terms).

## Observer-feedback loop

observe → discuss (positive + at-risk, two-way) → record card (de-identified) → aggregate / trend by
**behaviour category** → feed **systemic fixes** via `controls` + owned/dated `smart_actions` → close
the loop back to observers and the workforce. A card that produces no systemic learning, or that
names an individual for discipline, fails the de-id and hierarchy gates.

## How the skill uses this fragment

`bbs-program-designer` builds the observation card, the ABC routing rule, and the feedback loop from
this method, computes the metrics from `KB-DATA-BBS-METRICS` (with the `<5` small-cell guardrail),
and trends at-risk categories to hierarchy-ranked system fixes — never to the individual.
