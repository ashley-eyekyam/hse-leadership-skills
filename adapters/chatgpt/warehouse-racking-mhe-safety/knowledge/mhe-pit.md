# Powered industrial trucks / MHE — OSHA 1910.178 + PUWER + HSE L117 duty map

**Fragment ID:** `KB-REG-MHE-PIT`
**What this is:** a copyright-safe **duty → control map** for powered industrial
trucks (forklifts / MHE) and pedestrian-vehicle segregation in warehousing —
grounding `warehouse-racking-mhe-safety` (LOG-02). It names operator
competence/evaluation duties and the engineered segregation that must precede any
reliance on "look out for forklifts."
**What this is NOT:** a reproduction of OSHA 1910.178, PUWER 1998, HSE ACOP L117
or HSG136 text. Cite the regulation/ACOP numbers and the duty topics only.

> Source: OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178 (Powered Industrial Trucks) + PUWER 1998 + HSE ACOP L117 (Rider-operated lift trucks) + HSG136 (workplace transport) — citation map · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-23 · Volatile: true (guidance drift; refresh on review).

These instruments govern the safe selection, use, inspection and operation of
forklifts/MHE and the organisation of workplace transport so that vehicles and
pedestrians are kept apart. They ground the MHE/transport limb of a warehouse
safety artifact.

## Duty / clause → duty → control it grounds

| Clause / instrument | Duty (paraphrased) | Control it grounds |
|---|---|---|
| **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178(l)** | Operator training, evaluation and re-evaluation; only trained/authorised operators drive | operator training + competence evaluation record |
| **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178** (general) | Truck design/marking, daily pre-use inspection, defect removal-from-service | pre-use inspection log + defect process |
| **PUWER 1998** | Work equipment suitable, maintained, inspected; operator information/instruction | MHE maintenance + inspection regime (UK) |
| **HSE ACOP L117** | Rider-operated lift trucks — selection, operation, thorough examination | UK lift-truck operating regime |
| **HSE HSG136** (+ ANSI MH16.1 pointer for US rack design) | Workplace transport — keep vehicles and pedestrians apart by design | engineered segregation: barriers, one-way systems, marked/protected walkways and crossings |

## The control order (engineered segregation first)
Pedestrian-vehicle separation is achieved **by design first** — physical barriers,
segregated/one-way routes, marked and protected walkways and crossing points —
**before** relying on hi-vis clothing, signage or "look out for forklifts." A
warehouse artifact that treats MHE/pedestrian conflict with PPE/signage alone,
with no engineered segregation, is the indefensible output this map rejects
(see `KB-SNIP-RACKING-MHE`).

- Hierarchy of controls applies: segregation/engineering before admin/PPE.
