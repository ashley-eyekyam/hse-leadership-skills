# ISO 45003:2021 — Psychosocial Risk Management (guidance extending ISO 45001)

**Fragment ID:** `KB-STD-ISO45003`

> Source: ISO 45003:2021 (clause/topic structure only; user holds the licensed standard) · Year: 2021 · Reviewed: 2026-05-15 · Volatile: false (multi-year cycle).

ISO 45003 is psychosocial-risk **guidance extending ISO 45001** — it applies the
45001 management-system framework to psychosocial hazards. Its defining discipline:
controls must be **work-design controls, not just individual resilience** (the same
hierarchy-of-controls logic applied to how work is organised).

## Topic → what it grounds

| Topic | Requirement focus | Grounds (artifact) |
|---|---|---|
| Psychosocial hazard identification | aspects of work organisation, social factors, work environment | psychosocial hazard ID within a risk-assessment |
| Psychosocial risk assessment | likelihood/severity of harm to psychological health | psychosocial-RA |
| **Work-design controls** | **change the work, not just the worker** — workload, autonomy, role clarity, support (NOT PPE-equivalent "resilience training" alone) | control selection (ranked via `KB-SNIP-HOC`) |
| Monitoring & review | leading/lagging psychosocial indicators | board-safety-report psychosocial metrics |

## Usage note

Grounds psychosocial content and any psychosocial risk-assessment work. Pair with
`KB-STD-ISO45001` (45003 extends it) and apply `KB-SNIP-HOC` so psychosocial
controls are ranked work-design-first, flagging individual-resilience-only
recommendations as lower-order. See also `../research/psychosocial-hazards.md`.
