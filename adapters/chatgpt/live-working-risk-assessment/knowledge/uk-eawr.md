# United Kingdom — Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 (EAWR)

**Fragment ID:** `KB-REG-UK-EAWR`
**What this is:** a copyright-safe **regulation → duty → artifact** citation map
for the Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 (EAWR), with the supporting HSE
guidance HSR25 (the EAWR memorandum) and HSG85 (Electricity at work: safe
working practices). The discipline EAWR forces is **dead working as the default —
live work only where it is unreasonable to work dead AND it is reasonable to work
live AND suitable precautions are taken (reg 14's three-part test)**.
**What this is NOT:** a reproduction of the regulation or guidance text. Cite the
regulation numbers and topic structure only — never paste the wording.

> Source: Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 (SI 1989/635) + HSE HSR25 (memorandum of guidance) + HSG85 (safe working practices) — citation map · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-23 · Volatile: false (long-standing regulation).

EAWR places absolute and "so far as is reasonably practicable" duties on
electrical safety. The headline controls for power and utilities work are
isolation and proving dead (regs 12–13) and the live-work justification gate
(reg 14).
