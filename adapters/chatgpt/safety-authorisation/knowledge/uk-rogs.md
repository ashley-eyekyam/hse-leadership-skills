# United Kingdom — ROGS 2006 (Railways & Other Guided Transport Systems map)

**Fragment ID:** `KB-REG-ROGS`
**What this is:** a copyright-safe **duty → artifact map** for the Railways and
Other Guided Transport Systems (Safety) Regulations 2006 (ROGS) — the Safety
Management System (SMS) duties on transport operators and infrastructure
managers, the safety-authorisation / safety-certificate framework, and the risk
co-operation duties between dutyholders.
**What this is NOT:** a reproduction of the ROGS text or ORR guidance. Cite the
Regulation numbers and the duty topics only — never paste the statutory wording.

> Source: Railways and Other Guided Transport Systems (Safety) Regulations 2006 (SI 2006/599, as amended) + ORR guidance — citation map · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-21 · Volatile: true (statutory amendments + ORR guidance drift; refresh on review).

ROGS is the GB safety-regulation framework for mainline rail and other guided
transport (metros, trams, heritage). It requires dutyholders to operate a Safety
Management System and to hold the appropriate authorisation/certificate. It
grounds rail SMS, safety-case and risk-co-operation skills.

## Dutyholder / topic → duty → artifact it grounds

| Dutyholder / topic | Duty (paraphrased) | Artifact it grounds |
|---|---|---|
| Transport operator | Establish and maintain an SMS | the SMS document set |
| Infrastructure manager | Hold a safety authorisation for the infrastructure | safety-authorisation application + SMS |
| Transport undertaking | Hold a safety certificate for the operation | safety-certificate application + SMS |
| Risk assessment | Assess risks to safety from the operation | the operation's risk assessment |
| Co-operation | Co-operate so combined risks are controlled SFAIRP | inter-dutyholder co-operation arrangements |
| Safety-critical work | Manage competence + fitness of safety-critical staff | competence-management + fitness-for-duty records |
| Safety verification | Verify new/altered vehicles or infrastructure | safety-verification evidence |

## How the skill uses this fragment
- A GB rail skill resolves the dutyholder type first, then grounds the SMS element
  or authorisation/certificate artifact against the duty above.
- The "so far as is reasonably practicable" (SFAIRP) test governs control
  adequacy; controls follow the hierarchy of controls.
- A missing input (e.g., the live authorisation reference) is `[GAP]`, never
  invented.
