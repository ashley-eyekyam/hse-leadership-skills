# United States — OSHA 1910.269 (electric power generation, transmission & distribution)

**Fragment ID:** `KB-REG-OSHA1910-269`
**What this is:** a copyright-safe **duty → artifact** citation map for OSHA
29 CFR 1910.269 (the T&D standard) and the adjacent 1910.333 (selection and use
of work practices), framed as which electrical-power duty grounds which HSE
artifact. The signature discipline both standards force is **de-energize first,
establish an electrically safe work condition, before any reliance on PPE or
approach controls**.

> Source: OSHA 29 CFR 1910.269 (Electric Power Generation, Transmission & Distribution) + 29 CFR 1910.333 (Selection and use of work practices) — citation map · Year: 2026 · Reviewed: 2026-06-23 · Volatile: false (multi-year cycle).

OSHA 1910.269 governs work on generation, transmission, and distribution
installations; 1910.333 governs the safety-related work practices for energized
and de-energized equipment generally. The pair sets the **de-energize-first /
ESWC** discipline that defines defensible electrical-power safety work.
